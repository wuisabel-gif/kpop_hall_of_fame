# Kpop Crush

🇰🇷 [한국어로 읽기](README.ko.md)

I wanted a way to move through K-pop history that felt less like a Wikipedia table and more like walking somewhere. So I built one.

It opens in a dark hallway. Every group gets its own glowing doorway — Shinhwa near the start, KATSEYE at the far end — and you scroll up to walk past them, the lights changing colour as you cross from one generation into the next. K-Pop ON! plays while you go. Tap a doorway and it flips: debut date, agency, how many members, the fandom name. The photos are real, pulled live from Spotify.

Reach the end of the hall and you step into the catalogue — all five generations laid out, who debuted when, a 2026 tour board with dates and ticket links, and whatever just dropped.

## Run it

Open `index.html`. That's the whole thing — one file, no build, no install. Photos and music need a connection.

## A few honest notes

- The tour dates, ticket statuses, and "newest releases" are a June 2026 snapshot I filled in by hand. Treat them as a vibe, not gospel — check the real seller before you buy anything.
- Full songs only play if you're signed into Spotify in your browser. Otherwise you get 30-second previews. Spotify's rule, not mine.
- Where one generation ends and the next begins is a fan argument, not a science. NewJeans alone could start a fight. I drew the lines where most people draw them and kept walking.

Made for fun. Fork it, break it, add your own bias.
